Q: Design an Elevator System for a building with multiple elevators. 
Users can press buttons from floors or inside the elevator. 
System should assign the best elevator and handle requests efficiently.


1. Requirements Clarification (Speak Out)
✅ Ask these to the interviewer:
Do we have multiple elevators in the building? (Yes)
Are there external buttons (on floors) and internal buttons (inside elevator)? (Yes)
Should elevators optimize for shortest wait time? (Yes)


2. High-Level Design (HLD Overview - 2 min)
You can say:
I’d divide this into a central controller system that manages the elevators, 
processes requests, and assigns the best elevator. 
Each elevator has its own control logic (move, door open/close, etc). 
I’ll maintain a request queue, and status for each elevator.

I broke down the system into a controller-based architecture with each elevator maintaining its own queue. The scheduler dispatches based on direction and proximity. 
Movement and doors are simulated with a tick-based method or threads in actual systems.
Components:
ElevatorSystem (Controller) – Receives requests, assigns elevators
Elevators (Multiple) – Individual elevator units
Buttons (External and Internal)
Scheduler/Dispatcher – (Part of ElevatorSystem) assigns elevators based on logic
You can mention:
Use threads or simulate tick-based movement for elevators.
Min-heap/PriorityQueue can be used for closest elevator selection.
